Talking about the daily stand-up and it's three questions. In a typical. Scrum master asks the following three questions. What did you do yesterday? What will you do today? And are there any impediments in your way?

Let's accept. Some of the typical challenges these three questions may bring. First of all,. An engineer may tell you about valid stuff that he did yesterday, maybe even. Useful stuff. People may be feeling like they are doing a lot of work but that. Doesn't mean we are helping the team complete the sprint or deliver the.

Customer and minimum viable product any sooner. You also want to value people's. Time. You do not want the meeting to become a meeting where problems are.

But you want to get people not related to the problem out of the stand-up and. Back into using their time wisely. Then there is the overdoer or at least the. Person who wants people to know that he is doing a lot. Do you all know who. He is? It is fine if he wants to brag about how much he has achieved but.

In less than five minutes. Jeff Soderland, in his scrum, the art of doing. Twice the work in half of the time book, suggests the following three. Questions. What did you do yesterday to help the team finish the sprint? What. Will you do today to help the team finish the sprint?

What obstacles are. Getting in the team's way? Notice the focus on both the team and the success. Of the team in achieving the sprints goals. Hopefully these questions may. Help you to steer the conversation towards achieving your sprint goals. Team back into productivity.

Give them a try. Maybe print them in a poster and. Put them on this stand-up area. Thank you for watching. I am Jose Anes. Agile Scrum team.
